Who is Monet?


Oscar-Cluade Monet (1840-1926), widely known as Monet, was a French artist born on 14 November 1840 who founded the French Impressionism movement. Monet was a key figure in the founding of the movement and produced the most amount of paintings using Impressionism.

Throughtout his lifetime, Monet has painted a whopping amount of nearly 2000 paintings.
Monet desired to record the French countryside, leading him to paint the same scene repeatedly to express the change of light and seasons. Some subjects of his paintings were the Routhen Cathedral, Charing Cross Bridge, and the Water Lilies.



Monet's Technique


Having been described as "the driving force behind Impressionism". Monet’s career as a painter was mostly spent pursuing Impressionism.

In his lengthy career, Monet constantly painted the scenery and leisure activities of Paris and its environment including the Normandy Coast. 20th Century modernism was led by Monet by developing a special style that aimed to reproduce the very act of nature.

In his later works, Monet’s created a method of reproducing replicated studies of the same motif in series, switching canvases along with the light or along with his changing interests.

Starting in 1877, he painted a series of paintings of St-Lazare Station. Monet observed the way smoke and steam influenced visibility and colour. Sometimes, it would be opaque and sometimes it would be translucent. He wanted to use the study to observe and paint the influence of mist and rain on the landscape. His studies of the atmosphere would then develop into a hefty number of series of paintings where Monet replicated the same subjects in changing lights at different timings of the day,weather and season. The series he painted were regularly exhibited in groups. For example, his images of haystacks (1890-1891) and the Rouen Cathedral (1894).
