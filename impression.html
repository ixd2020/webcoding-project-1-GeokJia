What is Impressionism?

Impression,
Sunrise.

Oscar-Cluade Monet, widely known as Monet, was a French artist born on 14 November 1840 who founded the French Impressionism movement.

Monet was a key figure in the founding of the movement and produced the most amount of paintings using Impressionism.

Throughtout his lifetime, Monet has painted a whopping amount of nearly 2000 paintings.
Monet desired to record the French countryside, leading him to paint the same scene repeatedly to express the change of light and seasons. Some subjects of his paintings were the Routhen Cathedral, Charing Cross Bridge, and the Water Lilies.



Monet's Technique


Monet's floating studio


After exhibiting his paintings that used his painting method of using dabs,dashes and squiggles of paint in 1873,he had received harsh attacks from critic, Louis Leroy, who coined the term Impressionism named after one of Monet's painting, Impression,Sunrise with the intention of disparaging Monet and the other artists but the artists would appropriate the term for themselves instead.

The term surprsingly gained favour with the public, giving birth to what we know today as Impressionism.

Some central figures behind the Impressionist movement other than Monet were, Frédéric Bazille, Berthe Morisot , Gustave Caillebotte, Pierre-Auguste Renoir and more.
