Who is Monet?


Oscar-Cluade Monet, widely known as Monet, was a French artist born on 14 November 1840 who founded the French Impressionism movement. Monet was a key figure in the founding of the movement and produced the most amount of paintings using Impressionism.

Throughtout his lifetime, Monet has painted a whopping amount of nearly 2000 paintings.
Monet desired to record the French countryside, leading him to paint the same scene repeatedly to express the change of light and seasons. Some subjects of his paintings were the Routhen Cathedral, Charing Cross Bridge, and the Water Lilies.

Impressionism


Impressionism is a 19th century art movement defined by painting open compositions using small and thin yet visible lines that
emphasises on precise potrayal of light, normal subject matter, incorporation of movement as a important component of human
perception and experience, and uncommon visual angles.
